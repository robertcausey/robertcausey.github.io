03_Parasites

Introduction
In this module we will survey parasites, which are eukaryotic organisms, other than fungi, that use a host animal at some point in their life cycle. The group includes protozoa (single celled orgnanisms), helminths (tapeworms, flukes, round worms) and ectoparasites (mites, lice, and insects).

Ectoparasites
Mites
Mites are very small arachnids which complete their lifecycle on the host Otodectes cynotis - dog end cat ear mite (otodectic mange)
Notoedres cati - scabies in cats (notoedric mange)
Sarcoptes scabei var canis - a.k.a scabies - sarcoptic mange in dogs. Contagious and transmissible to humans, but self-limiting i.e. cannot complete life cycle.
Cheyletiella blakei (cats) and Cheyletiella yasguri (dogs) highly contagious walking dandruff.
Demodex canis - Demodectic mange. Localized in puppies, but can be generalized in immunosuppressed puppies and adulta. Normally part of the local flora i.e. not contagious.

Ticks
Ticks are arachnids larger than mites in the subclass Acari. They are Major transmitters of disease. They include
Dermancentor variabilis American Dog tick
Dermacentor andersoni the Rocky Mountain wood tick. Both of these can spread Rickettsia rickettsii Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever and other diseases.

Amblyomma e.g. lone star tick. Also transmits RMSF, and Babesia.
Transmit Borrelia burgdorferi (Lyme disease), Anaplasmosis
